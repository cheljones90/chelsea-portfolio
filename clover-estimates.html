Clover Estimates

Designing a native estimates feature end-to-end for Clover's professional services merchants, from discovery through MVP launch and beyond.

TL;DR

The problem: Clover's professional services merchants were losing work to competitors because they had no native way to send estimates, forcing them to stitch together 2–3 tools for one job.

01

Problem & Background

Merchants were stitching together two disconnected tools to do one job.

The Gap

Professional services merchants (contractors, consultants, tradespeople) don't just take payments. They scope work, send estimates for client approval, collect deposits, then invoice for the remainder. Clover had invoicing, but no estimates.

That gap forced merchants to use QuickBooks, HoneyBook, or Square for the first half of every job, then switch back to Clover to collect payment. The friction was costing Clover merchants. Competitors who owned the full workflow were winning.

The goal: Close the loop from scoping work through to receiving payment, all within Clover, without merchants ever needing to leave the platform.

Key Design Decision

Building something new instead of bending something that already existed.

The Challenge

We wanted to let merchants add custom fields to estimates, a way to include project-specific information for clients, like project codes, SKUs, or work order numbers.

Clover already had a custom fields feature. But its existing behaviour was to collect information from the customer, used in hosted checkout and virtual terminal. Repurposing it would have broken a familiar pattern that merchants already relied on, and would have created confusion about when to use which version.

The Options
Reconsidered

Repurpose existing custom fields

Modify the existing feature to support both collect and provide behaviours. Faster to build, but it breaks a familiar merchant pattern and introduces a mode-switching concept that adds cognitive load.

Chosen Direction

Net-new "provide information" behaviour

A new custom fields implementation scoped to estimates, with a single purpose: providing information to the client. Cleaner mental model, no risk of breaking existing patterns, and merchants can search records by those fields.

Why It Matters

The distinction between collect and provide sounds subtle, but for merchants managing dozens of active jobs, it's the difference between a tool that's intuitive and one that requires constant re-learning. Advocating for a cleaner mental model, even when it means more engineering effort, leads to better products and less user confusion. The proposal was approved and is currently in merchant testing.
